02 · Context

Regology was a regtech platform that used AI to help financial institutions track and comply with thousands of regulations across multiple jurisdictions simultaneously. I joined as the sole product designer and owned the UX end-to-end, from user research and wireframes through to a full design system and developer handoff.

03 · Problem

The problem space.

Professional compliance users had sophisticated needs the product wasn’t yet meeting.

No jurisdiction-scoped search

Users couldn’t search within a specific jurisdiction; every query returned global results.

H·01 Visibility

Alert noise with no filter

78 unread alerts with no way to route, prioritise, or mute irrelevant feeds.

H·04 Consistency

Invisible regulation hierarchy

The parent–child relationship between CFR → Part → Section had no visual representation.

H·06 Recognition

Version changes without context

When regulations updated, there was no diff view to show what actually changed.

05 Buttons & controls: every interactive trigger, and where it belongs
Primary Action
The one true action on a screen, wearing the brand purple so the eye finds it instantly.
Include, Save, Apply Filter, the thing you most want clicked.Never more than one per view, and never for anything destructive.
Secondary Action
A soft-purple companion for the supporting action that sits beside the primary.
View details, open a panel, secondary confirm.Don’t pair two of these, one will always quietly lose.
Ghost Utility
Outlined and quiet, present when you look for it, invisible when you don’t.
Export, Cancel, low-stakes utilities.Not for the main action, it’s too shy to lead.
Danger Destructive
Stays calmly outlined, then floods red on hover, a beat of friction before regret.
Exclude, Delete, anything you can’t easily undo.Never the default focus, make people reach for it.
Icon button Utility
A 36×36 square for toolbar triggers where a word would crowd the row.
Filter, kebab menu, compact toolbars.Never without a tooltip, a lonely icon is a guess.
IncludeExclude
Action chips Action
Include / Exclude living inside a row or card, the lightweight cousins of the big buttons.
Per-item include & exclude in tables and reg cards.Not for page-level actions, promote those to buttons.
Segmented toggle View switch
Flips between equal ways of seeing the same data, with one always lit.
Table ↔ Card. Two or three peers, max.Not for filtering, that’s what tabs are for.
Filter tabs View switch
Scopes a list in place, same screen, narrower slice.
Include / Unread / Exclude states of a result set.Don’t mix with navigation, tabs stay on one page.
Switch Toggle
Instant on/off with no Save in sight, the change happens the moment it flips.
Decompose mode, Track changes, live preferences.Not when the choice needs an explicit confirm step.

09 · Reflection

What I learned.

  • 01Designing for experts means reducing steps, not simplifying. Compliance officers don’t want less information, they want better navigation.
  • 02Iteration with the client is a design tool. The v1→v6 journey wasn’t rework; it was how we discovered what the product actually needed to be.
  • 03The patterns we built in 2019, hierarchy visualisation, change diffs, smart alert routing, became the core of Regology’s 2026 AI agent architecture.
